# The Pact of the Realms (Code of Conduct)

## The Oath

As architects, scribes, and seekers of the Voix artifact, we pledge to ensure the Sanctuary remains open to all who seek knowledge and contribute to its perfection. We vow to make participation a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity, wisdom, rank, or origin.

We pledge to forge code and interact in ways that honor the realms—keeping our community healthy, diverse, and secure.

## The Standards of Ascension

Behavior that strengthens the Forge includes:

* Channeling empathy and kindness toward fellow seekers.
* Listening respectfully to differing philosophies and techniques.
* Offering and gracefully accepting constructive critiques on code and design.
* Acknowledging when a rune is flawed, apologizing to those affected, and learning from the shatter.
* Focusing on the fortification of the entire community over individual glory.

Behavior that shatters the Sanctuary is strictly forbidden:

* Any use of sexualized language, imagery, or unwarranted advances.
* Antagonism, insults, trolling, and political/personal attacks.
* Harassment, whether in the open courtyards or whispered in private messages.
* Exposing another's physical coordinates or electronic addresses without express consent (doxxing).
* Any other conduct that brings disrepute to a professional, unified endeavor.

## Enforcement of the Pact

The Elders (Community Leaders) are bound by duty to clarify and enforce these standards. They hold the authority to banish, alter, or reject comments, commits, pull requests, and any bindings that threaten the Code of Conduct. The Elders will communicate their judgements with fairness and reason.

## Scope of the Artifact

This Pact governs all aspects of the Voix community—from the internal repositories to any realm where an individual represents the project (such as speaking at communal gatherings or corresponding via official project channels).
